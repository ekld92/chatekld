# Hypertension — First-line Management

For most adults with uncomplicated hypertension, first-line therapy includes a
thiazide diuretic, an ACE inhibitor, an angiotensin receptor blocker, or a
calcium channel blocker. Beta-blockers are not recommended as initial
monotherapy unless there is a compelling indication such as recent myocardial
infarction.

A blood pressure target of below 130/80 mmHg is recommended for most patients.
